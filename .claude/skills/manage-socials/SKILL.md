# Manage Socials

This skill helps Wen manage their public presence across four main channels: Substack, LinkedIn, X (Twitter), and YouTube.

## Context
Wen writes about AI, product management, the shift from PM to founder, and building sustainable businesses (Easein). The tone is reflective, insightful, professional but warm, and direct.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Content Ideation & Drafting
When Wen provides a topic or a rough draft:
1. **Identify the primary channel**: Is this a long-form Substack post, a professional LinkedIn update, a short X thread, or a YouTube video script/description?
2. **Draft the core message**: Use `references/voice.md` to ensure the tone matches Wen's style. Focus on the "second half of life" theme (creating vs. learning) and the realities of being an AI-powered founder.
3. **Format for the channel**:
   - **Substack**: Long-form, narrative-driven, clear headings.
   - **LinkedIn**: Engaging hook, concise paragraphs, professional insight, 1-2 relevant hashtags.
   - **X (Twitter)**: Punchy, thread format if needed, highly direct.
   - **YouTube**: Engaging title, clear description with links to DMPtech.ai and Easein, timestamps if applicable.

### Step 2: Review & Refine
1. Present the draft(s) to Wen for review.
2. **NEVER** publish or finalize without explicit approval from Wen.
3. Ask for specific feedback: "Does this capture your voice? Is the hook strong enough?"

### Step 3: Cross-Pollination
Once a piece of content is approved for one channel, automatically suggest adaptations for the others:
- If a Substack post is approved, draft a LinkedIn summary and an X thread summarizing the key points.
- If a YouTube video is planned, draft a promotional X post and LinkedIn update.

### Step 4: Asset Management
If images or links are needed, ensure they point to the correct resources (e.g., easein-6053.myshopify.com, dmptech.ai, or substack.com/@thewenge).
